"Nityannadhanam - the will of service that gives hope to every hunger."

Nityannadhanam

✅ Poor & Needy People– Those who struggle for daily meals.

✅ Visitors & Devotees – People visiting the ashram for spiritual and seva activities.

✅ Sadhu & Sannyasis – Saints and seekers who follow spiritual paths.

✅ Ashram Residents – Elderly, orphans, and other dependents staying in the ashram.

✅ Cows & Animals – As part of Goseva, ensuring proper nourishment.

Food Serving & Distribution

✅ Organizes community meals or free meal programs at the ashram.

✅ Maintains a seating arrangement for smooth serving.

✅ Assigns volunteers to ensure food reaches every person without discrimination.

✅ Records daily meal counts for effective planning.

Example: If 300 people visit daily, the system calculates food portions accordingly to minimize waste.

Kitchen & Cooking Management

✅ Uses Goshala’s dairy products (milk, ghee, curd) for Sattvic meals.

✅ Prepares meals in a traditional, hygienic manner using organic ingredients.

✅ Follows Ayurvedic and Sattvic food guidelines to ensure balanced nutrition.

✅ Uses fresh, organic, and chemical-free ingredients.

✅ Prepares special meals during festivals using cow-based products like panchagavya.

Example: Daily meals include chapati, dal, rice, buttermilk, and cow ghee, while festive meals include payasam made with Goshala milk.

Donation & Fundraising Management

✅ Organizes donations (cash, rice, vegetables, groceries, etc.).

✅ Maintains donor records, contributions, and receipts.

✅ Conducts fundraising events and digital donation campaigns.

✅ Ensures transparent usage of funds for food procurement.

Example: If a devotee donates ₹10,000 or 100 kg of rice, the system records it and updates stock levels.
